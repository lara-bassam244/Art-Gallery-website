About

1904 - 1929

Salvador Dalí was born on May 11th, 1904, in Figueres, Catalonia, Spain. In 1922 he moved to Madrid to study at the Royal Academy of Fine Arts of San Fernando.

In 1929 he met Gala, who becomes his lifelong companion and muse.

1931 - 1948

In 1931, Dalí gained international acclaim with his debut solo exhibition in Paris, showcasing iconic works including "The Persistence of Memory" (1931) and "Soft Construction with Boiled Beans (Premonition of Civil War)" (1936).

In 1948, he published 'The Secret Life of Salvador Dalí,' providing insights into his eccentric personality and artistic philosophy.

1950-1989

Dalí explored religious and scientific themes in artworks such as “Crucifixion (Corpus Hypercubus)” and “Galatea of the Spheres.” In the 1970s, his declining health affected his artistic productivity.

Gala's death in 1982 deeply impacted him, and Dalí passed away from heart failure on January 23rd, 1989, in Figueres, Spain.
